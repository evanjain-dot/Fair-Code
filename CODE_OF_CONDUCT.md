# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

FairCode exists to challenge bias — in algorithms and in people.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Just as this project works to remove unfair bias from predictive systems, we hold ourselves to the same standard in how we treat each other.

---

## Our Standards

**We encourage:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language in issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints, methodologies, and interpretations of fairness
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive criticism — especially on technical approaches
- Centering the conversation on ideas and evidence, not on personal disagreements
- Acknowledging the limits of your own perspective; this work touches on race, justice, and systemic harm — approach it with humility
- Crediting sources, datasets, and prior work honestly

**We do not tolerate:**

- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting remarks, or personal attacks
- Dismissing concerns about bias or harm as "just technical" or "not a real issue"
- Publishing anyone's private information without their explicit consent
- Claiming credit for others' contributions

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any related communication channels. It also applies when someone is representing this project publicly.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainer:

**Yash Kewlani** — via GitHub: [@yakew7](https://github.com/yakew7)

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled with discretion. Retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith is itself a violation of this Code.

Consequences for violations may include a warning, temporary restriction from the project, or permanent removal, depending on the severity and pattern of behaviour.
